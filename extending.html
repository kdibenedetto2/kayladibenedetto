Extending & Reflecting

Extending our Project


The final piece of our project was to implement an additional feature to our prothestic hand. Jake and I settled on creating a hand-mounted mirror that would screw onto place on the top of the hand, but could also fold up and down in order to be flat along the hand. The idea behind this addition was that users lacking a hand would be unable to hold a mirror using the protheses as it exists in its current state (grip is not ideal in this iteration for holding small objects), and thus, also would be unable to adjust their appearance at the same time. Our idea allows our users to hold out their hand and flip up the mirror, then change their appearance however they want with their free hand. Reflecting on our Project


This project was one that was very satisfying and rewarding to me. It honestly felt pretty easy to create something that could prove to be really helpful to those missing a hand or living with some other sort of disability that affects their hands. Like we've discussed in class, the ethics of design must take into account the real life experiences of the users, not just the aesthetic or technological goals of the designer. Connecting to our class reading, "What Can a Body Do?", particularly on page 14 of the reading, the author asks, "Which way is the best way to make an object that functions but one that also counts, one that can elicit a story bigger than its parts? How will the object work, but also: Why will it matter? Andwhere shall we begin?". These questions resonate a lot with Project 4 because I feel at times, we become so caught up in doing everything we can to make sure our design translates into a working object, but we must also keep in mind whether it contributes to something bigger than itself in a meaningful way. Our hands did just that, and the e-NABLE organization does a stellar job of emphasizing this sentiment. The role of rapid prototyping, as implemented in this project, makes these accessible creations more reachable to the general public and cost effective for everyone involved, including both the manufacturer and user.
